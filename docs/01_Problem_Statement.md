# Problem Statement

## Overview

Financial inclusion remains one of the biggest challenges in emerging economies, particularly in India. While digital payments and online financial services have expanded rapidly, a significant portion of the population still remains underserved by traditional financial institutions.

According to the hackathon problem statement, over **190 million adults in India are credit invisible**, meaning they have little or no formal credit history. Since conventional lending systems primarily rely on credit bureau scores, individuals without an established financial record often struggle to access loans, credit cards, or other financial services despite demonstrating responsible financial behaviour in their daily lives.

At the same time, millions of first-time investors, particularly from Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities, face challenges in understanding investment products due to financial jargon, lack of personalised guidance, and limited financial literacy. This creates barriers to long-term wealth creation and informed financial decision-making.

CreditMiners aims to address both of these challenges through a unified AI-powered platform that promotes financial inclusion and investment awareness.

---

# Background

Traditional credit scoring systems primarily depend on historical borrowing behaviour.

These models work effectively for users who have previously interacted with banks or financial institutions. However, they fail to evaluate individuals who have never taken loans or used credit products.

Many users consistently demonstrate responsible financial behaviour through activities such as:

- Regular mobile recharges
- Timely utility bill payments
- Consistent digital payment usage
- Responsible online shopping behaviour

Although these activities reflect financial discipline, they are generally ignored by conventional credit scoring mechanisms.

Similarly, investment advisory platforms often assume prior financial knowledge, making them difficult for first-time investors to understand and trust.

# Problem Statement

The project addresses two interconnected problems:

## Problem 1: Credit Invisibility

Millions of users remain excluded from formal lending systems because they lack traditional credit history.

This prevents deserving individuals from accessing financial opportunities.

---

## Problem 2: Investment Accessibility

First-time investors often struggle to:

- Understand financial terminology
- Assess investment risks
- Select suitable investment instruments
- Build diversified portfolios

As a result, many potential investors avoid investing altogether.

---

# Objectives

The objectives of CreditMiners are:

- Design an alternative credit scoring engine using non-traditional financial signals.
- Generate transparent and explainable credit scores.
- Recommend actionable improvements for increasing creditworthiness.
- Assess investment risk through a conversational questionnaire.
- Provide educational micro-investment recommendations.
- Promote financial inclusion through accessible AI-powered tools.
